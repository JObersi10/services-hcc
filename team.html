Our Story

From a school computer lab
to client projects.


Hack Club Curaçao started as an after-school club at a handful of high schools in Willemstad — a group of students teaching each other to code because nobody else was. What began as weekend tutorials turned into building real websites for family businesses, then local non-profits, then paying clients.

Today we're a registered non-profit. Every client project is staffed by students, reviewed by peer "leads" with more experience, and quality-checked before delivery. The money clients pay covers software subscriptions, hardware for new members, and travel to regional hackathons — nothing else.

We rotate roles often on purpose. A member who designs this quarter might write backend code next quarter. The goal isn't to specialise early — it's to leave with a real, broad foundation in how digital products get made.
